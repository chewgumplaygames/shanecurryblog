Why This Exists

Raw notes are often where the useful specificity lives. The publishing goal is not to polish that material into marketing copy. The goal is to preserve the original idea, clarify its scope, separate observation from inference, and publish it at a stable URL.

Workflow

  1. Capture a raw note about one main topic.
  2. Extract the main claim and define the key terms.
  3. Separate production knowledge from theory.
  4. Publish the result as a static post or article with visible dates and metadata.

Formatting Standard

  • Literal title
  • One-paragraph summary near the top
  • Visible author and dates
  • Explicit caveats
  • Related repos or demos when available
  • JSON-LD that matches the visible page

Current State

The scaffolding for this workflow lives in the project templates. The first real article pages should be created only after raw thought material is available.

Published thought chunks will appear on /blog/. More durable topic references can also be linked from /animation/.
